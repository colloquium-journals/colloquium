# Code of Conduct

Colloquium is committed to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and productive academic community. This code of conduct applies to all participants in our platform, including authors, reviewers, editors, and readers.

## Our Values

### Respect and Inclusivity
- Treat all community members with dignity and respect
- Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- Use inclusive language in all communications
- Respect cultural, linguistic, and disciplinary differences

### Academic Integrity
- Maintain the highest standards of scholarly honesty
- Provide constructive, evidence-based feedback
- Acknowledge contributions and sources appropriately
- Report suspected misconduct through proper channels

### Professional Conduct
- Engage in civil, productive discourse
- Focus criticism on ideas, methods, and findingsâ€”not individuals
- Maintain confidentiality during the review process
- Disclose conflicts of interest transparently

## Expected Behavior

### For All Participants

**Do:**
- Communicate respectfully and professionally
- Provide constructive feedback aimed at improving research
- Respect confidentiality and review timelines
- Report violations of this code when witnessed
- Support a welcoming environment for all

**Don't:**
- Use discriminatory, harassing, or offensive language
- Make personal attacks or ad hominem arguments
- Share confidential review materials
- Misrepresent data, methods, or findings
- Abuse the platform or attempt to circumvent policies

### For Authors

**Responsibilities:**
- Submit original, honest research
- Respond professionally to reviewer feedback
- Acknowledge all contributors and funding sources
- Provide necessary data and materials for verification
- Correct errors promptly when identified

### For Reviewers

**Responsibilities:**
- Provide timely, thorough, and constructive reviews
- Maintain confidentiality of manuscripts under review
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Focus on scientific merit and methodology
- Offer specific, actionable suggestions for improvement

### For Editors

**Responsibilities:**
- Ensure fair and unbiased review processes
- Maintain editorial independence
- Handle conflicts of interest appropriately
- Provide clear guidance to authors and reviewers
- Uphold the highest standards of editorial ethics

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered violations of our code:

### Harassment and Discrimination
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Stalking or following (online or offline)

### Academic Misconduct
- Plagiarism or unauthorized use of others' work
- Fabrication or falsification of data
- Duplicate or redundant publication
- Inappropriate authorship practices
- Failure to disclose conflicts of interest

### Platform Abuse
- Spamming or flooding discussions
- Sharing confidential review materials
- Creating multiple accounts to circumvent restrictions
- Attempting to manipulate the review process

## Reporting and Enforcement

### How to Report

If you experience or witness a violation of this code:

1. **Direct Response**: Address minor issues directly when safe and appropriate
2. **Platform Reporting**: Use our built-in reporting tools for platform-related issues
3. **Editorial Contact**: Email conduct@colloquium.org for serious violations
4. **Anonymous Reporting**: Use our anonymous reporting form for sensitive matters

### What to Include

When reporting a violation, please provide:
- Description of the incident
- Names of individuals involved (if known)
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Any supporting documentation
- Your preferred outcome or resolution

### Investigation Process

1. **Initial Review**: Reports are reviewed within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Thorough investigation by appropriate personnel
3. **Response**: Action taken based on severity and circumstances
4. **Follow-up**: Check-in with affected parties
5. **Documentation**: Maintain records for pattern identification

### Possible Consequences

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation:

- **Warning**: Formal notice of policy violation
- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access to platform features
- **Content Removal**: Deletion of violating posts or comments
- **Review Restrictions**: Limitation of review privileges
- **Account Suspension**: Temporary or permanent ban from platform
- **Editorial Action**: Retraction or correction of published work

## Appeals Process

If you believe enforcement action was taken in error:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Email appeals@colloquium.org within 30 days
2. **Provide Evidence**: Include relevant documentation
3. **Review Process**: Independent review by appeals committee
4. **Decision**: Final determination communicated within 14 days
