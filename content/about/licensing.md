# Licensing & Open Access

Colloquium is committed to open access publishing, ensuring that research is freely available to everyone. This page explains our licensing policies, author rights, and reuse permissions.

## Open Access Policy

### Gold Open Access
All articles published in Colloquium are immediately available under open access terms:
- **Free to read**: No subscription or paywall barriers
- **Free to download**: Complete articles in multiple formats
- **Free to share**: Liberal sharing and distribution rights
- **Free to reuse**: Clear permissions for educational and research use

### No Author Fees
Unlike many open access journals, Colloquium does not charge:
- Article Processing Charges (APCs)
- Submission fees
- Review fees
- Publication fees
- Color figure charges

Our open-source, community-driven model eliminates financial barriers for authors.

## Creative Commons Licensing

### Default License: CC BY 4.0

Unless otherwise specified, all content is licensed under [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/).

**This means you are free to:**
- **Share**: Copy and redistribute in any medium or format
- **Adapt**: Remix, transform, and build upon the material
- **Commercial use**: Use for any purpose, including commercial
- **No additional restrictions**: Licensor cannot revoke these freedoms

**Under the following terms:**
- **Attribution**: Must give appropriate credit and indicate if changes were made
- **No warranties**: Material provided "as-is" without warranties
- **Compliance**: Must comply with license terms and applicable law

### Alternative Licensing Options

Authors may choose from several Creative Commons licenses:

**CC BY-SA 4.0 (Share-Alike)**
- Same permissions as CC BY
- Derivative works must use same license
- Ensures continued openness of adaptations

**CC BY-NC 4.0 (Non-Commercial)**
- Prohibits commercial use without permission
- May limit impact and reuse
- Generally not recommended for research

**CC BY-ND 4.0 (No Derivatives)**
- Prohibits creation of derivative works
- Limits adaptation and translation
- May reduce research impact

### License Selection Process
1. Authors select preferred license during submission
2. Editorial team may recommend alternative if appropriate
3. License clearly displayed with published article
4. License applies to all versions and formats

## Author Rights and Responsibilities

### Rights Retained by Authors

**Copyright Ownership**
- Authors retain copyright to their work
- No copyright transfer to Colloquium required
- Authors can republish and reuse their own work

**Distribution Rights**
- Share pre-prints and post-prints freely
- Deposit in institutional repositories
- Post on personal websites and social media
- Include in future compilations or books

**Modification Rights**
- Create derivative works from their own research
- Translate into other languages
- Adapt for different audiences
- Update and revise as needed

### Author Responsibilities

**License Compliance**
- Ensure chosen license is appropriate
- Understand implications of license selection
- Inform co-authors of licensing decisions
- Comply with funder requirements

**Third-Party Content**
- Obtain permissions for non-original content
- Provide proper attribution for all sources
- Ensure rights to license all included materials
- Clear documentation of permissions obtained

**Accuracy and Updates**
- Maintain accuracy of published information
- Provide corrections when necessary
- Update contact information as needed
- Respond to legitimate reuse requests

## Data and Code Licensing

### Research Data

**Default Policy**
- Encourage open data practices
- Support standard data repositories
- Recommend permissive licensing
- Require data availability statements

**Data Licenses**
- CC0 (Public Domain) recommended for datasets
- CC BY acceptable for documented datasets
- Restrictive licenses only when necessary
- Clear documentation of access conditions

### Software and Code

**Open Source Software**
- Encourage sharing of analysis code
- Support standard repositories (GitHub, GitLab)
- Recommend OSI-approved licenses
- Provide citation guidance for software

**Recommended Licenses**
- MIT License for maximum compatibility
- Apache 2.0 for patent protection
- GPL for copyleft requirements
- BSD for academic flexibility
