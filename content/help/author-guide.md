# Author Guide

This comprehensive guide will help you submit your research to Colloquium and navigate the open review process.

## Before You Submit

### Manuscript Preparation
- Format your manuscript according to our guidelines
- Ensure all data and code are available (when applicable)
- Review our ethical guidelines and policies
- Prepare any supplementary materials

### Submission Requirements
- Original research that hasn't been published elsewhere
- Compliance with ethical standards
- Proper attribution and citations
- Clear, accessible writing

## Submission Process

### Step 1: Create Your Account
1. Register for a Colloquium account
2. Complete your profile with ORCID and institutional affiliation
3. Verify your email address

### Step 2: Prepare Your Submission
1. Upload your manuscript file (PDF, Word, or LaTeX)
2. Add title, abstract, and keywords
3. Select appropriate subject categories
4. Include any supplementary files

### Step 3: Submit for Review
1. Review all information for accuracy
2. Agree to our submission terms
3. Submit your manuscript
4. Receive confirmation email

## The Review Process

### Open Review System
Unlike traditional journals, Colloquium uses an open review process where:
- Reviewer identities are transparent
- Review conversations are visible to the community
- Authors can respond to reviewers directly
- The process encourages constructive dialogue

### Review Timeline
- **Initial screening**: 1-2 weeks
- **Peer review**: 4-6 weeks
- **Author response**: 2-4 weeks
- **Final decision**: 1-2 weeks

### Participating in Review Conversations
- Respond professionally to reviewer comments
- Ask clarifying questions when needed
- Provide additional data or explanations
- Acknowledge valid criticisms constructively

## After Publication

### Promotion and Sharing
- Share your published work through social media
- Add the publication to your CV and profiles
- Engage with community discussions about your work

### Post-Publication Review
- Respond to post-publication comments
- Participate in ongoing scholarly discussions
- Consider writing response articles or corrections if needed

## Tips for Success

1. **Write clearly**: Make your work accessible to a broad audience
2. **Be responsive**: Engage actively in the review process
3. **Stay professional**: Maintain respectful dialogue with reviewers
4. **Provide context**: Explain the significance of your work
5. **Be transparent**: Share data and methods when possible

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I submit work that's on a preprint server?**
A: Yes, we accept submissions that are available as preprints.

**Q: What if I disagree with a reviewer?**
A: You can respectfully respond with your perspective and evidence.

**Q: How long does the review process take?**
A: Typically 8-12 weeks from submission to decision.

**Q: Can I withdraw my submission?**
A: Yes, you can withdraw at any time before publication.
